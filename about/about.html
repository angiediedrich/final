About Snook Inn

Originally, the "Snook Hole," named for the abundance of Snook right off the dock, the Snook Inn has been a landmark on Marco Island for over 30 years, an island far different than what you see today.

The current owners purchased the property in 1985. Their Snook Inn has evolved into an internationally known, casual, waterfront, full-service, restaurant, with ample docking facilities for your visit by boat.

Marco Island is the largest of Florida's Ten Thousand Islands, located on the Gulf of Mexico in Southwest Florida. History says nothing about Marco Island until 1870 when settlers appeared here, the W. T. Collier family at the North end of the Island, and the Barfields at the South end. The pioneer period began in earnest! Clam factories, schools the Post Office and even a railroad came to Marco Island along with increasing population.
